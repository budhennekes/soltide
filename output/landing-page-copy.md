## Problem

**Header:** You spend 90% of your day under the wrong light.

Indoor light is too dim to wake your body up in the morning and too bright to let it wind down at night. So you feel groggy at 9 AM, wired at 11 PM, and confused about why.

Sleep trackers tell you what happened last night. They don't tell you what to do about it at 7 this morning.

---

## Solution / How It Works

**Header:** Three light moments a day. Soltide times them for you.

**1. 🌅 Morning light**
Sunlight within an hour of waking is the strongest signal you can give your body clock. Soltide tells you the exact window for your location, down to the minute.

**2. 🌞 Vitamin D**
Your skin can only make vitamin D when the sun is high enough. Soltide shows you when that window opens and closes today, wherever you are.

**3. 🌙 Wind-down**
After sunset, bright light pushes your body clock later. Soltide tells you when to dim the lights based on your bedtime, so sleep comes easier.

Open the app, read one line, step outside. That's the whole routine.

**Header:** Glanceable in three seconds.

- **One answer up top.** "Get outside now. Best time: now, 10 to 20 minutes, until 8:30 AM." No charts to interpret, no data to decode.

## Objections

**Header:** Built calm on purpose.

- **"Another app that nags me?"** Reminders are off by default. Turn on the ones you want, up to three a day, never during sleep.
- **"Do I need an account?"** No. Nothing to sign up for, nothing to cancel. Your settings and check-ins stay on your phone.
- **"Is my location being tracked?"** Soltide uses your location once to calculate sun times, on your device. You can type a city instead and never share it at all.
- **"I already use a sleep tracker."** Keep it. Trackers measure sleep; Soltide helps you earn it during the day. They work well together.

## Notes on choices

- The headline teaches the premise ("your body runs on light") because most visitors don't know circadian timing is actionable. Clarity over cleverness.
- Every section gives a concrete time or number (90% indoors, 3 seconds, 10 to 20 minutes, 6:38 sunrise) because specificity is what separates this from generic wellness copy.
- The final CTA header should be dynamic if the page is built: pull tomorrow's real sunrise time for the visitor's location. It turns the close into a live demo of the product.
- "No streaks, no guilt" does double duty: it differentiates from Duolingo-style habit apps and pre-empts the "another thing to fail at" objection.
- Compliance-safe throughout: no absolute claims, no cure language, "helps" and "supports" framing, disclaimer included.
