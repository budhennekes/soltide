# Sol · the right light at the right time

Your body runs on light. Sol tells you when to get it: one calm recommendation at a time, timed to the actual sun where you live.

**[Try it live](https://budhennekes.github.io/sol-circadian/)** · free, no account, works on your phone

## Why this exists

Indoor light is too dim to wake your body up in the morning and too bright to let it wind down at night. So you feel groggy at 9 AM, wired at 11 PM, and confused about why. Sleep trackers tell you what happened last night. They don't tell you what to do about it at 7 this morning. Sol does: open it, read one line, step outside.

## What it does

- **One answer up top.** "Get outside now. Best time: now, 10 to 20 minutes, until 8:30 AM." No charts to interpret.
- **Your whole day on one arc.** Sunrise, sunset, where the sun is right now, and today's three light windows beneath it.
- **Vitamin D, actually timed.** The window opens when the sun is high enough at your location, tuned to your skin type, closed seasons handled honestly.
- **Gentle tracking, no guilt.** Tap once when you catch a light moment. Three small suns fill as your day does. Tomorrow resets on its own; there are no streaks to break.
- **Reminders your way.** Browser notifications where supported, or a two week calendar file with your real sunrise times on iPhone.
- **Private by default.** No account. Settings and check-ins stay on your phone. Type a city instead of sharing location if you prefer.

## Getting started

Open **[budhennekes.github.io/sol-circadian](https://budhennekes.github.io/sol-circadian/)** on your phone. In Safari, tap Share, then **Add to Home Screen**. Sol opens full screen like a native app.

Personalize it in Settings: wake time and bedtime shift your light windows to match your real day.

## How it's built

One HTML file, no build step, no backend. Sun position is calculated on your device; weather and UV come from [Open-Meteo](https://open-meteo.com/). Append `?sim=13:00` to the URL to preview any time of day.

## Fine print

Sol offers general wellness guidance based on sunlight, UV, weather, and time of day. It does not provide medical advice or diagnose or treat vitamin D deficiency.
